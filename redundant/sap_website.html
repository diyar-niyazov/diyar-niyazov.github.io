STEM

Science, technology, engineering, mathematics, computer science, and research careers.

Sample careers

  • Software developer or cybersecurity analyst
  • Mechanical, civil, electrical, or aerospace engineer
  • Data scientist, statistician, or AI specialist
  • Biologist, chemist, physicist, or lab researcher

Education path

  • Strong Algebra II, Precalculus, Calculus, and statistics base
  • Physics, chemistry, biology, computer science, or engineering electives
  • Associate, bachelor's, certificate, or graduate degree depending on role
  • Portfolio, GitHub, lab experience, internships, or design projects

Start now

  • Join Robotics, Science Olympiad, Math League, or coding clubs
  • Build apps, websites, experiments, CAD models, or engineering prototypes
  • Ask to shadow engineers, IT staff, lab techs, or local researchers
  • Take online programming, electronics, or data-analysis mini courses

Skills to build

  • Quantitative reasoning and careful troubleshooting
  • Technical writing, documentation, and presentation skills
  • Team collaboration on long, complex projects
  • Curiosity, persistence, and comfort with testing ideas

Healthcare

Patient care, medicine, rehabilitation, public health, diagnostics, and biomedical careers.

Sample careers

  • Physician, physician assistant, nurse practitioner, or nurse
  • Physical therapist, occupational therapist, or athletic trainer
  • Pharmacist, medical lab scientist, radiology tech, or sonographer
  • Biomedical engineer, public health analyst, or health administrator

Education path

  • Biology, chemistry, anatomy, psychology, statistics, and health science
  • Licensure or certification for many clinical and technical roles
  • Certificate, associate, bachelor's, master's, doctoral, or medical degree options
  • Clinical hours, volunteering, shadowing, and patient-care experience

Start now

  • Volunteer at hospitals, clinics, senior centers, or community health events
  • Explore EMT, CNA, first aid, CPR, or lifeguard certification routes
  • Shadow healthcare workers and ask about daily responsibilities
  • Take anatomy, biology, chemistry, psychology, and statistics seriously

Skills to build

  • Empathy, calm communication, and professional responsibility
  • Attention to detail with safety, records, and procedures
  • Scientific reasoning and comfort with medical terminology
  • Stamina, teamwork, and ethical decision-making

Business

Finance, accounting, marketing, management, entrepreneurship, analytics, and hospitality.

Sample careers

  • Accountant, financial analyst, banker, or investment associate
  • Marketing strategist, sales manager, brand manager, or event planner
  • Entrepreneur, operations manager, supply-chain analyst, or consultant
  • Hospitality manager, sports business professional, or real estate agent

Education path

  • Business, economics, accounting, statistics, communications, and computer apps
  • Associate or bachelor's degree for many roles; MBA for advanced leadership
  • Certifications can help in accounting, finance, analytics, and project management
  • Internships, student businesses, competitions, and leadership experience matter

Start now

  • Join DECA, student government, yearbook business staff, or fundraising teams
  • Track a mock investment portfolio or create a small student-run business
  • Practice spreadsheets, presentations, budgeting, and market research
  • Interview local business owners about operations, customers, and pricing

Skills to build

  • Clear communication, negotiation, and professional writing
  • Financial literacy, data analysis, and decision-making
  • Leadership, organization, and accountability under deadlines
  • Creative problem solving with customer and community needs in mind

Public service

Government, law, public safety, military, emergency response, policy, and nonprofit work.

Sample careers

  • Attorney, paralegal, judge, policy analyst, or city planner
  • Firefighter, EMT, police officer, dispatcher, or emergency manager
  • Military officer, intelligence analyst, diplomat, or public administrator
  • Nonprofit manager, community organizer, or environmental policy specialist

Education path

  • Government, history, economics, English, statistics, and communications
  • Academies, civil-service exams, military training, college, or law school
  • Public administration, criminal justice, political science, or legal studies routes
  • Background checks, fitness standards, internships, and service hours may apply

Start now

  • Join student government, Model UN, debate, mock trial, or community service clubs
  • Volunteer with local nonprofits, public events, fire departments, or EMS programs
  • Attend town meetings and learn how local decisions are made
  • Practice writing policy briefs, speeches, and research summaries

Skills to build

  • Judgment, integrity, confidentiality, and responsibility
  • Public speaking, research, writing, and conflict resolution
  • Crisis management, teamwork, and calm decision-making
  • Understanding laws, institutions, communities, and civic needs

Education and social sciences

Teaching, counseling, psychology, human development, social work, and student support.

Sample careers

  • Elementary, middle, high school, special education, or CTE teacher
  • School counselor, psychologist, social worker, or speech-language pathologist
  • College professor, academic advisor, instructional designer, or coach
  • Human services specialist, researcher, or youth-program coordinator

Education path

  • Psychology, sociology, child development, English, and communication courses
  • Teaching certification, student teaching, graduate programs, or licensure
  • Research methods and statistics for psychology and social science tracks
  • Experience tutoring, mentoring, coaching, or leading student groups

Start now

  • Tutor peers, assist younger students, coach, or volunteer at youth programs
  • Observe how great teachers explain ideas and manage classrooms
  • Take psychology, sociology, public speaking, and writing courses
  • Build patience by helping others through difficult learning moments

Skills to build

  • Empathy, listening, patience, and inclusive communication
  • Lesson planning, feedback, organization, and adaptability
  • Research, observation, and ethical decision-making
  • Leadership that helps people feel capable and respected

Humanities

History, philosophy, language, journalism, writing, cultural studies, and research careers.

Sample careers

  • Journalist, editor, technical writer, copywriter, or communications specialist
  • Historian, archivist, librarian, museum curator, or cultural resource manager
  • Translator, interpreter, linguist, foreign service worker, or researcher
  • Ethics consultant, policy researcher, grant writer, or legal professional

Education path

  • English, history, world languages, philosophy, government, and media courses
  • Bachelor's degree for many paths; graduate study for archives, libraries, or academia
  • Portfolio of writing, research, translation, editing, or media work
  • Internships with newspapers, museums, libraries, nonprofits, or public offices

Start now

  • Write for the school newspaper, literary magazine, website, or club newsletter
  • Study a world language and practice real conversation or translation
  • Visit museums, archives, libraries, and local historical societies
  • Build a writing portfolio with essays, articles, interviews, and reviews

Skills to build

  • Research, reading comprehension, source evaluation, and synthesis
  • Writing, editing, interviewing, and public presentation
  • Cultural awareness, ethical reasoning, and historical thinking
  • Curiosity about people, systems, stories, and ideas

Creative fields

Art, design, media, performance, architecture, animation, music, writing, and production.

Sample careers

  • Graphic designer, UX/UI designer, illustrator, animator, or photographer
  • Filmmaker, video editor, musician, actor, producer, or audio engineer
  • Architect, interior designer, industrial designer, or fashion designer
  • Creative director, content strategist, game artist, or social media producer

Education path

  • Art, music, theater, media, photography, CAD, design, and technology electives
  • Portfolio-based admissions for many art, design, film, and architecture programs
  • Certificate, associate, bachelor's, conservatory, apprenticeship, or freelance routes
  • Software skills in Adobe, Figma, CAD, DAWs, editing tools, or 3D programs

Start now

  • Create a portfolio site with your strongest projects and process notes
  • Join theater, band, art club, media club, yearbook, or morning show teams
  • Offer design, photo, video, or audio help to clubs and local groups
  • Practice giving and receiving critique without taking it personally

Skills to build

  • Visual judgment, storytelling, composition, and craft
  • Software fluency, production planning, and deadline management
  • Client communication, revision habits, and creative resilience
  • Originality balanced with audience, purpose, and constraints

Trades and skilled labor

Hands-on technical careers in construction, manufacturing, repair, service, and applied design.

Sample careers

  • Electrician, plumber, HVAC technician, welder, carpenter, or machinist
  • Automotive technician, diesel mechanic, aviation mechanic, or robotics technician
  • Culinary professional, cosmetologist, barber, landscape technician, or contractor
  • Construction manager, building inspector, equipment operator, or maintenance lead

Education path

  • BOCES, CTE programs, apprenticeships, trade school, union training, or military training
  • Math, physics, technical drawing, business, safety, and hands-on shop skills
  • Licenses and certifications vary by trade and state
  • Paid apprenticeships often combine classroom learning with supervised work

Start now

  • Explore BOCES, CTE, shop, robotics, automotive, culinary, or design programs
  • Learn tool safety, measurement, blueprints, wiring basics, or repair fundamentals
  • Ask local tradespeople about apprenticeship timelines and daily work
  • Build small projects that show precision, patience, and care

Skills to build

  • Safety habits, reliability, punctuality, and physical awareness
  • Measurement, spatial reasoning, troubleshooting, and tool fluency
  • Customer service, teamwork, and clear job-site communication
  • Pride in craft, quality control, and continuous improvement
The team

Meet the founders

SAP was built by Diyar Niyazov, Sam DiNolfo, and Tyler Kenny with a shared mission to make academic resources accessible to everyone.

Student Assistance Program

The founders created SAP as a student-built hub for academic prep, career exploration, club discovery, and peer support at Victor High School.

The goal is simple: make the information students usually have to hunt for easy to find, easy to understand, and useful when planning the next step.
